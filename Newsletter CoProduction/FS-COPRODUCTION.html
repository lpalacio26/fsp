Post Tenebras Lux
Film

Premiere
Cannes FF (2012)
Awards
Cannes FF – Best Director
Mar del Plata FF – Best Latin-American Film Special Mention
Mexico, France, Netherlands, Germany
2K | 115 min
Juan and his family decide to leave the pace and comfort of city life for the Mexican countryside, where their lifestyle appears in stark contrast to that of the local community. Things begin to unravel for the mother and father as their seemingly idyllic dynamic shifts from tender to brutal. They enjoy and suffer a world apart. Nobody knows if these two worlds are complementary or if they strive to eliminate one another.
POST TENEBRAS LUX ("light after darkness") presents the viewer with both a restrained domestic tale and a series of unforgettable impressionistic images, capturing a family’s place within the sublime yet unforgiving natural world. The provocative feature won Reygadas the Best Director Award at the Cannes Film Festival.
“Mesmerizing, mysterious, willfully perverse.” – Manohla Dargis, New York Times

Battle In Heaven (Batalla en el Cielo)
Film

Premiere
Cannes FF – Competition (2005)
Restoration: Berlinale – Classics (2024)
Awards
FIPRESCI Award - Rio De Janeiro FF
Mexico, France, Germany, Belgium
4K Dolby Atmos | 98 min
Marcos and his wife kidnap a baby for ransom money, but it goes tragically wrong when the infant dies. In another world is Ana, the daughter of the general he drives for, who prostitutes herself for pleasure. Marcos confesses his guilt to her in his troubled search for relief. And then finds himself on his knees amidst the multitude of believers moving slowly towards the Basilica in honour of the Lady of Guadalupe.
4K restoration supervised by the director from the original negative.

Japón
Film

Premiere
Rotterdam IFF (2002)
Awards
Cannes FF – Golden Camera – Special Mention
Mexico, Netherlands, Germany, Spain
2K | 130 min
A man leaves Mexico City for the remote countryside, in search of a place to end his life. He finds himself lodging in the rickety home of an old Indian widow, overlooking a magnificent canyon. In the vast wilderness, he is confronted with the boundless humanity of his host, his dulled senses return to him, reawakening sexual desires and other instincts. JAPÓN is a meditative tale about regaining a lost life, beautifully shot in Super CinemaScope with natural light.
JAPÓN is Carlos Reygadas first feature film. The 2K restoration by Criterion was supervised and approved by the director.
“Criterion has graced us with an intoxicatingly beautiful release of a strange and challenging film.” – Keith Watson, Slant Magazine

Carlos Reygadas
Carlos Reygadas was born in 1971 in Mexico. His first feature film, JAPÓN was presented in 2002 at the film festivals in Rotterdam and in Cannes where it received the Camera d'Or. BATTLE IN HEAVEN (2005), his second feature film, premiered in competition at Cannes. SILENT LIGHT (2007) won the Jury Prize in Cannes, while POST TENEBRAS LUX (2012) earned Reygadas the Best Director Award at Cannes.
